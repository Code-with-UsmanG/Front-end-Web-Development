Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, the Father of the Green Revolution.
Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist who is known for his work in improving agricultural productivity.

His research led to the development of high-yielding varieties of staple crops such as wheat and rice, which played a significant role in alleviating hunger and food insecurity in many parts of the world.

Borlaug's work earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970, recognizing his contributions to global food security.
